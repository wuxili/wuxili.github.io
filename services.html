Tiny Transactions on Computer Science (TinyToCS)

  • Program Committee Chair, Vol. IV
  • Program Committee Member, Vol. III
TinyToCS is the premier venue for Computer Science research papers of 140 characters or less. It has three primary merits:
  • It advocates a new publication medium that relies on social networks (Follow @TinyToCS) and mobile platforms to reach a broad mass of people
  • It provides an open platform for timely, inter-discipline discussions that foster new and creative research ideas
  • It is completely originated and organized by students.

Emerging Topics in Computer System Design Seminar

Matthew Halpern and I co-founded the Emerging Topics in Computer System Design reading group at UT Austin with the following three purposes:
  • To unite computer system students at UT Austin to form a student community
  • To keep up with cutting-edge research in computer system design
  • To practice public technical speech.

Women in Engineering Program (WEP)

I am actively involved in the Women in Engineering Program, which is a UT Austin student service for all engineering women aims at providing them academic, career, and leadership opportunities.
  • Mentored two female sophomores in WEP's Graduates Linked with Undergraduates in Engineering (GLUE) Program
  • Speaker at high school-focused summer camps to encourage high school female to pursue STEM careers.
